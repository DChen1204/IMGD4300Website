Explaination:

The particle effect I have created is raining asteroids. The effect consists of hundreds of asteroid particles constantly raining down. The asteroids spawn in varying sizes and velocities. Asteroids have trails behind them as they drop. The website allows three controls – spawn rate, trail length, and gravity. Spawn rate controls how many asteroids is being created; trail length is obvious; gravity allows the asteroids to stop falling or fall crazy fast at the extremes. I used the position history trail to create the trail effect. The trails are 20 previous frames of the asteroid scaled down. This project is meant to be a practice for not using gulls, so it is relatively easy on the design part. However, the technical aspect is complicated and main.js is cluttered with setup codes. I think I can develop more complex graphics in the future as I gain more experience.

The raining asteroid is inspired by Phainon’s skill “Foundation: Stardeath Verdict” from Honkai Star Rail. The original effect shows amazing graphics, and I can feel it is a devastating attack. I tried to emulate it with my best effort. I tried to simulate the flames using my fire effect from my WebGPU Intro assignment, but it did not look well. The incredible fire effect around the asteroid of the original effect is out of my skill range, so I chose a simplistic design to keep the asteroids aesthetically pleasing. The asteroid has a black core, a flame layer, and a fade layer. The fade layer is made more transparent for the glowing effect. The animation appears to have near and far layers. The near layer has larger asteroids falling at higher speed, which provides a visionally stunning effect. The far layer has smaller and slow asteroids that acts as an interesting background for detailed observations.
