Explaination:

Tech - I mainly used the time function to change the positions of the lines in various ways. To draw the shapes, I created the drawShape function that takes in the pixel position, the rectangle position, and rectangle size. In drawShape, I defined the four boundaries of the rectangle and use step() to check if the pixel is within the rectangle. In the end, the pixel will be colored if it is within any shapes. The colored rectangles use mix() to color themselves. To create the animation, I had many equations to get the desired movements across multiple phases.

Aesthetic - I was inspired by the painting Piet Mondrian - Tableau shown in The Book of Shaders. I recreated the painting in shader and I was motivated to make it come alive through movement. The animation starts off slow with the lines starts moving in the same direction one by one. Then it hits the first climax when the horizontal lines and vertical lines are moving at the same time and changing direction. The animation slows down when the horizontal lines starts bouncing around, but then quickly picks up when the vertical lines bounce too. In the end, the rotation breaks the translation theme and the random spin creates the ultimate chaos. The transition of the painting from orderly to chaotic fascinates me.
